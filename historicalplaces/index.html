Qutub Minar

Qutub Minar in Delhi, India was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1993 for its Historical significance and architectural brilliance. The red sandstone tower, which part of the Qutub Minar complex, was bulit in early 13th Century.

Chola Temples

The Great living chola temples, a group of Hindu temples in Tamil Nadu, were first inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1987 with inclusion of the Brihadisvara temple at Thanjavur. The other two temples in the group were added in 2004. In 2004, the name was also changed from 'Brihadisvara Temple', Thanjavur to Great Living Chola Temples.

World Heritage Site

Ajanta Caves

Since 1983, Ajanta caves have been listed among the UNESCO World Heritage Sites of India. The caves are Located in Aurangabad and are a protected Monuments of the Archaelogical survey of India. They are made of rock and contain Buddhist sculptures and paintings that depict the life of Lord Buddha.

World Heritage Site

Khajuraho Temples

The Khjuraho Group of Monuments in Madhya Pradesh, India has been a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1986. The temples are known for their erotic sculptures, nagara-style architrcture, and spiritual teachings. The chandela dynasty bulit most of temples between 950 and 1050 AD.

World Heritage Site

Elephanta Caves

The Elephanta Caves in Mumbai, India were designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1987 for their Historical and cultural significance. The caves feature artwork that depicts themes from Hinduism's Shaktism and Vaishnavism traditions, including Shiva, Nataraja, and mount Kailash.

World Heritage Site

Fatehpur Sikri

Fatehpur Sikri in Uttar Pradesh, India was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site 1986. The town was founded in 1569 by Mughal emperor Akbar and complex of places, residences, and religious and official buildings.

Agra Fort

The Agra Fort in uttar pradesh, india is a UNESCO World heritage sites. It was addes to the list in 1983 and is located about 2.5 kilometers northwest of Taj Mahal. The fort is made of red sandstone and includes the mughal rulers city and mosques.

1. Which are Famous historical places in South india?

a) Virupaksha Temple, Karnataka:

Amongest the oldest historical sites in India, the Virupaksha Temple in Hampi is said to be nothing short of a marvel. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, where you can see sculputres of Lord shiva inteicately carved on stones.The temple was built during the rule of Deva Raya II of the vijayanagr Empire.

b) Mysore Palace, Mysore:

One of the most famous historical places in India is the Mysore palace, in the heart of the city of Mysore. I built in 1912 by a british architect Lord Henery, in the Indo Saracenic style of architecture, the Mysore Palace boasts of intricate interios, opulent rooms and stunning decor.

c) Brihadisvara Temple, Thanjavur:

Located in the city of Thanjvur, in Tamil Nadu, the Brihadisvara Temple is amongst the largest and oldest temples in the country, in addition to being an exemplary example of the Hindu style of architecture.

02. Which are famous historical places in North India?

a) Qutub Minar, Delhi:

Qutub Minar is one of the most famous ancient places in India. It is made out of red sandstone in the Iranian style of architecture, and is considered to be one of the tallest structure ever in Indian history. The inscriptions engraved on the tower also tell a lot about its history.

b) Red Fort, Delhi:

Another one of the famous Historical places located in North India is the Red Fort or Lal Qila, which stands as a testimony to the glory and power of the Mughal Sultanate. It is also a fine example of the blend of the Persian and other styles of architecture, and has many opulent rooms and others attractions within it.

c) Fatehpur Sikri, Agra:

When visting North India, one of the best historical places that you can go to Fatehpur Sikri, located a few kilometres to the west of Agra. It was the former capital of Akbar, and is home to the buland Darwaza, which is the highest gateway in the World.

3. What are the top five monuments of India?

The top five monuments of India include the Taj Mahal, a stunnign structure made out of white marble, located in Agra. This is followed by Golden Temple in Amritsar, which is the holiest shrine and most famous pilgrimage sites for the Sikhs, and Thanjavur's Brihadeeswara Temple, which is one of the largest temples in the country and dedicated to Lord Shiva.

The top five monuments of India also include Jaipur's Hawa Mahal, which was constructed for the women of royal families, and the victoria Memorial in Kolkata, which was constructed as a dedicated to Queen victoria

Most Asked Questions on India

  • What is the Best Season to visit in India?

    India Can be visited at any time throughout the year, However, Winter experiences pleasant weather and Summers are too hot and humid. One can visit beach destinations like Goa, Pondicherry, Andamans in Winter along with some heritage sights like Hampi, Ajanta & Ellora and the desert state of Rajasthan. Hill stations are suitable for escaping the scorching heat of summer and waterfalls are best visited in monsoon.

    The most visited destinations in India are Delhi, Agra, Jaipur, Shimla, Manali, Coorg, Ooty, Munnar, Goa, Pondicherry, Nainital, Mahabaleshwar, Udaipur, Dwarka, Somnath, Madurai, Ajanta Caves, Gangtok, Darjeeling.

  • What are the top Heritage circuits in India?

    There are different heritage circuits spread across India. The most Popular being Golden Triangle (Delhi, Agra, Jaipur) from Delhi. Another circuit starting from Mumbai - Aurangabad - Ajanta Caves and Ellora Caves can be visited. Another popular Circuit includes Hampi - Badami - Pattadakal from Bangalore or Hubli. In Tamilnadu, Mahabalipuram - Kanchipuram and Pondicherry circuit and Madurai - Thanjavur- Trichy are popular heritage circuits.

  • What are the popular Pilgrimages circuits in India?

    There are different pilgrimage circuits spread across India. The most popular being Haridwar - Rishikesh from Delhi. Another circuit starting from Mumbai or Pune and visit Nashik - Shirdi - Trimbakeshwara can be visited. Another popular circuit includes Dwarka - Somnath from Ahmedabad. Tamilnadu has some wonderful pilgrimage circuits like Madurai - Rameshwaram - Kanyakumari. Karnataka pilgrimage circuit includes Kukke - Udupi - Murudeshwar -Gokarna. Andhra has several pilgrimage sites, the popular circuit is Tirumala - Tirupati - Srikalahasti

  • What are the most visited hill stations in India?

    The most visited hill stations in India are Coorg, Ooty, Munnar, Shimla, Manali, Kodaikanal, Nainital, Mussoorie, Gangtok, Darjeeling, Mahabaleshwar and Lonavala.
